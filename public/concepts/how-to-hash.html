A Brief History of Hash
Origins
One of the oldest cannabis
preparations in human history.

Hash predates modern flower consumption by centuries. The desire to isolate the most potent part of the plant is ancient — what's changed is how precisely we can do it.

Ancient
Central Asia &
Middle East
Charas — the original concentrate

The earliest recorded hash was made by hand-rubbing live cannabis plants, collecting the sticky resin on the palms, and pressing it into dark, dense slabs. This method — called charas — is still practiced in parts of India and Nepal today, passed down through generations without much change.

Traditional
Morocco &
Afghanistan
Kief pressing & regional styles

In Morocco, dry-sift kief pressing became the standard — dried cannabis sifted through fine mesh screens, pressed into pale crumbly blocks. Afghanistan and Lebanon developed their own distinct styles, each with recognizable textures and effect profiles that spread through trade routes into Europe and the West.

Evolution
The Modern
Movement
Same goal, better tools

The modern concentrate movement is a direct evolution of this ancient practice — just with a much better understanding of chemistry and far more precise equipment. Ice water extraction, rosin pressing, closed-loop hydrocarbon systems. All different methods. All the same core goal: isolate and concentrate the plant's most valuable compounds.

Today
Craft
Concentrates
The premium tier

Live rosin, hash rosin, full-melt bubble hash. The same ancient principle — collect the resin — refined to a level of purity and flavor complexity that wasn't possible until recently. The legal market demands consistency and safety testing. The craft movement demands something more: that the product actually tastes like the plant it came from.

What changed is the how. What didn't change is the what — it's still about trichomes. It's still about resin.
3
The science, simply
Why concentrates are stronger.

Cannabis plants produce trichomes — tiny mushroom-shaped glands that coat the flowers. Inside those trichomes are the compounds that make cannabis work: cannabinoids like THC and CBD, and terpenes that shape flavor and aroma.

When you smoke flower

You're burning the whole plant — cellulose, chlorophyll, plant waxes, and all. Only a fraction of what you're consuming is the active compounds you're after.

When you dab a concentrate

Everything except the active compounds has been removed. What remains is almost entirely cannabinoids and terpenes — which is why concentrates are stronger, cleaner-tasting, and more efficient per unit.

Why people make the switch
Efficiency
Less is more

A fraction of the amount for the same or stronger effect. Concentrates are 60–90%+ THC versus 15–25% in typical flower.

Flavor
Terpene clarity

Terpenes are more pronounced, complex, and varied than in flower. Premium concentrates taste like the plant — not the smoke.

Experience
Noticeably different

Done right, it's a qualitatively different effect. More nuanced, more tied to the specific cultivar and its terpene profile.

The Dab Rig — Step by Step
Section 02 · The classic setup
How to dab, done right.

The traditional method. A water pipe with a heated quartz banger instead of a bowl. Requires a torch and a little patience — especially on step 4.

What you need: dab rig · quartz banger · torch · dab tool · carb cap · cotton swabs · isopropyl alcohol
1
Fill with water
Add enough water that it bubbles when you draw, but doesn't splash into the stem. About 1–2 inches in most rigs.
2
Prepare your dab
Use a dab tool to pick up a small amount — grain of rice or smaller to start. Set it aside on parchment or the edge of your tool.
3
Heat the banger
Hold the torch 2–3 inches from the banger. Heat the bottom and sides for 20–30 seconds until the quartz begins to glow faintly.
4
Wait — this is the most important step
Set a timer. For live rosin: 50–60 seconds. For standard BHO: 35–45 seconds. Lower temp = better flavor, smoother hit.
The 60-second rule for rosin
5
Load the concentrate
Place concentrate into the banger — it should vaporize immediately. If it combusts or turns black, it was still too hot.
6
Cap it & inhale slowly
Place the carb cap over the banger. Draw gently — a slow, controlled pull is more effective and more comfortable.
7
Swab while warm
While the banger is still warm, swab inside with a dry cotton swab, then an ISO-dipped one. A clean banger = better flavor every time.

Temperature — The Single Most Important Variable

Heat it right.

At high temps, terpenes combust before they reach you. At 450–520°F they vaporize cleanly — preserving the full character of the concentrate.

Terpenes — Flavor, Aroma & Effect
Section 04 · Why terpenes matter more than THC
Don't just read THC.
Read the terpenes.

Terpenes are the aromatic compounds responsible for flavor, aroma, and the specific character of effects. A concentrate with a rich terpene profile at 70% THC is almost always a better experience than stripped distillate at 90%.

Anything above 0.5% terpene content is noticeable. Above 1% is significant. The most flavorful concentrates often run 3–5% total terpenes.
How to read a label

When you see a terpene percentage on a lab test, you're seeing how much is present by weight. 0.5%+ is noticeable. 1%+ is significant. The best concentrates often run 3–5%+.

Terpenes + temperature

Myrcene and limonene start vaporizing around 334–348°F — the first to burn off at high heat. Another reason low-temp dabbing matters.

The entourage effect

Cannabinoids and terpenes work better together than in isolation. A rich, diverse terpene profile produces a more complex, more effective experience than high-THC product with stripped-out terpenes.

Storage — Protecting What You Paid For
Section 06 · Storage
Sealed. Cool. Dark.

Concentrate degrades when exposed to heat, light, air, and moisture. Avoiding them is simple once you know what to watch for.

Air
Keep sealed between every use
Even a few minutes of air exposure degrades terpenes. Never leave lids loose while setting up.
Light
Store away from sunlight & UV
Don't leave on a sunny countertop or near a window. UV light accelerates degradation significantly.
Heat
Keep below 75°F consistently
Never store in a warm car, near a heat source, or in a hot bathroom. Rosin in a hot car can degrade in hours.
Moisture
Use dry, sealed containers only
Fridge condensation contaminates product. Always let cold containers reach room temp before opening.
Short-term
Days to
2 weeks
Sealed glass or silicone · room temperature
A sealed container in a cool drawer is all you need. Under 75°F. Silicone is easy to scoop from; glass is better for flavor — silicone absorbs trace terpenes over time.
Medium-term
2 weeks to
2 months
Refrigerator · sealed, in a bag or box
Keep at the back of the fridge, away from odors and moisture.
Warm-up rule: remove 20–30 min before use. Open only after container feels like room temp.
Long-term
Months
Freezer · airtight containers only
Nearly halts degradation. Let frozen product thaw completely before opening — may take an hour or more.

Cleaning — Protect Your Gear, Protect Your Flavor
Section 07 · Cleaning
A dirty banger ruins expensive product.

Residue from previous sessions burns alongside fresh concentrate, introducing char flavor and permanently staining your quartz. Clean gear isn't optional — it's the difference between tasting your product and tasting yesterday's.

After every dab
The 60-second habit
01
While the banger is still warm — not hot — take a dry cotton swab and wipe the inside walls in a circular motion.
02
Follow with a swab lightly dipped in 99% isopropyl alcohol. This dissolves residue the dry swab missed.
03
Let cool completely before your next dab. ISO evaporates — but don't torch a banger that's still wet.
Residue comes off easily while warm. Don't wait.
Deep clean — banger
Weekly for regular consumers
01
Place the banger in a zip-lock bag or glass jar. Cover completely with 99% isopropyl alcohol.
02
Soak 30–60 minutes. Stubborn staining can go overnight.
03
Rinse thoroughly with warm water, dry completely. Never torch a wet banger.
04
For persistent staining: torch until residue burns off, then swab while warm.
99% ISO only — 70% won't cut concentrate residue
Clean the rig
Every 1–2 weeks
01
Empty the water. Pour in enough 99% ISO to coat all internal surfaces — about a quarter full.
02
Add a pinch of coarse salt. Cover all openings and shake vigorously for 30–60 seconds.
03
Pour out the ISO, rinse thoroughly with warm water several times until no ISO smell remains. Air dry.
Reclaim in the rig restricts airflow and adds stale flavor
Terp pearls & carb cap
After every few sessions
01
Soak terp pearls in 99% ISO for 20–30 minutes. Rinse, dry completely before use.
02
Clean pearls spin better and distribute heat more evenly — the difference is noticeable.
03
Wipe the carb cap inside and out with an ISO-dipped swab. Residue on the cap reheats and affects flavor.
Clean pearls = noticeably better low-temp performance

Common Mistakes — And How to Fix Them
Section 08 · What goes wrong
Most bad dabs are predictable.

None of these are hard to fix once you know what to watch for.

1
Using too much
A grain of rice — or smaller. Trust the size. Take one, wait 15 minutes. There is no experience ruined by a dab that was too small.
2
Dabbing too hot
Set a timer after torching. 45 seconds minimum, 60 for rosin. The banger may not look ready — but it's at a better temperature.
3
Skipping the carb cap
Not optional. Without it, concentrate can't vaporize fully at low temps. You leave product and flavor behind every single dab.
4
Chasing THC % over terpenes
72% live rosin with a rich terpene profile almost always beats 90% distillate. Shop by profile, not percentage.
5
Skipping gear maintenance
Swab after every dab while the banger is still warm. Deep clean with 99% ISO weekly. A dirty banger ruins expensive product.
6
Storing carelessly
Sealed. Cool. Dark. Rosin in a hot car can degrade in hours. Fridge for 2+ weeks, freezer for months — warm up before opening.

Words Worth Knowing — The Glossary
Section 09 · Glossary
The words that matter.

Every term on a concentrate menu, explained plainly.

Trichomes
Tiny crystal glands on cannabis flowers where all the value lives — THC, CBD, terpenes. Concentrates are essentially concentrated trichomes.
Terpenes
Aromatic compounds responsible for flavor, aroma, and effect character. Over 200 identified in cannabis. They interact with cannabinoids to shape your experience.
Entourage Effect
Cannabinoids and terpenes working better together than in isolation. Why full-spectrum and solventless products are valued over isolates.
Solventless
Made using only water, heat, and/or pressure — no chemical solvents. Bubble hash and rosin are solventless. The cleanest extraction method.
Live (Rosin / Resin)
Made from fresh-frozen plant material — frozen immediately after harvest. Preserves terpenes that would otherwise degrade during drying.
Banger / Nail
The heated quartz cup on a dab rig where concentrate is applied. Quartz retains heat evenly and doesn't affect flavor.
Carb Cap
A cap placed over the banger after loading concentrate. Restricts airflow, retains heat. Essential for low-temp dabbing — not optional.
Terp Pearls
Small quartz balls that spin inside the banger under the carb cap, distributing heat and concentrate evenly. A simple upgrade with real impact.
Decarboxylation
Heat-activated conversion of THCA to THC — happens automatically when you dab or vape. This is what makes the product psychoactive.
Reclaim
Residue that collects inside a rig after multiple dabs. Can be reused though flavor is diminished. Clean your rig regularly to prevent buildup.
